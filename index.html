What is a GovTech Cohort?
A government agency defines a real operational problem. RIN publishes it as an open national challenge. 5 startups are selected, spend 12 weeks building a solution with the government customer in the room, and pitch at Demo Day. The winner gets a real paid pilot contract.
Why Inland Empire?
Riverside County is the 4th largest county in California with 2.4M residents, growing at 3% annually vs the state's 0.4%. It has NSWC Corona (3,900 engineers), March ARB (9,600 employees), Cal Fire Southern Region HQ, and the World Logistics Center (40.6M sqft) — all within 30 miles of each other.

Why Not an Incubator?
Riverside already has 9+ innovation centers: ExCITE, OASIS Park, IE Tech Bridge, UCR EPIC SBDC, Murrieta Innovation Center, TVE2, Pass Business Hub, Palm Desert iHub, Coachella Valley iHub, SCEIN. Building another would duplicate what already exists. RIN coordinates above them.
IT IS
  • The single front door for government customers
  • The layer that connects existing centers to each other
  • The entity that runs government challenge cohorts
Five Core Functions
01
SINGLE FRONT DOOR
One contact point for all government and industry partners
02
GOVTECH COHORTS
Agency problems. 12-week sprints. Pilot contracts.
Different from a Pitch Competition?
Pitch competitions give startups $2,500–$30,000 in prize money and send them home. RIN's challenge cohorts give startups a government customer in the room for 12 weeks and end with a signed pilot contract worth $250K–$1M+. The customer is present from day one, not at the end as a hoped-for buyer.
03
FEDERAL GRANT COORDINATOR
EDA Tech Hubs · NSF Engines · Defense funds
04
STARTUP ROUTING
GovTech intake · Defense pipeline · Warm handoffs
05
GRADUATE RETENTION
One scoreboard · One metric · Regional talent
Why This Single Metric?
Every program RIN runs — challenge cohorts, grant applications, startup routing — ultimately asks the same question: are educated people staying in Riverside to work? That is the scoreboard. If the number goes up over 3 years, the program is working. If it doesn't, everything else is theater.

Real Problem Example
Cal Fire needs real-time crew location tracking that works without cell towers during active wildfire suppression. Current radio systems fail in terrain. GPS devices are expensive and disposable. A startup that solves this for Cal Fire has a product immediately relevant to fire agencies in 44 US states.

The SBIR Bridge
SBIR and STTR are federal programs that give $150K–$2M grants to early-stage companies doing government-relevant tech work. The 12 weeks of work a non-winning team did IS their SBIR Phase 1 application — already built, already documented. RIN staff help them file it within 30 days of Demo Day. A 'loss' at Demo Day becomes a federally funded next step.

What the EPA Actually Enables
The UCR-NSWC Corona EPA enables joint research proposals, data sharing, student internships at NSWC Corona, and faculty collaborations on defense research. Between 2021-2025, the Navy awarded UCR over $400K in Naval Engineering Education Consortium grants. 169 UCR alumni now work at NAVSEA.
Defense Vertical Elements
OTA — Other Transaction Authority
Faster than standard FAR procurement. NSWC Corona can issue OTA agreements directly to startups. Other federal agencies can piggyback — one Riverside contract reaches 30+ federal sites.
Why OTA Matters for Startups
Standard federal procurement (FAR-based) takes 18-36 months and requires extensive past performance documentation. Most startups cannot participate. OTA agreements are not subject to FAR, can be competed in weeks, and allow rapid prototyping with real government customers. NSWC Corona already uses them via the IE Tech Bridge Crucible program.
IE Tech Bridge & Crucible
Inland Empire Tech Bridge anchored by NSWC Corona. 18 startups launched. 23 in the pipeline. The defense startup infrastructure already exists — RIN connects it to county.
Dual-Use Technology
Civilian applications with defense readiness. The overlap between government operational problems and commercial markets is where the most durable companies form.
Navy Tech Bridge Network
30+ national nodes. Technology proven at Corona has built-in national distribution to every other Tech Bridge — and from there into broader DoD procurement.
The National Distribution Math
If a startup wins an OTA pilot contract at NSWC Corona, that technology has a pathway to 30+ Navy Tech Bridge nodes nationally. Each node connects to local defense contractors, university partners, and federal installations. One Riverside contract, properly structured, can generate procurement conversations at naval installations from Norfolk to Pearl Harbor.

WLC in Context
The $25 billion World Logistics Center spans 2,600 acres in Moreno Valley, making it the largest planned logistics and business park in North America. It is also designed to be the largest net-zero GHG logistics development in the US. The facility sits between the Ports of LA/Long Beach and the rest of the continent — making it the natural testing ground for any technology that improves how goods move through the US supply chain.
Logistics Vertical Elements
Cooperative Purchasing — NASPO & OMNIA
National Association of State Procurement Officials cooperative vehicles. One Riverside County adoption puts a solution on a contract that every county in America can access. No new RFP. No new sales cycle. One sale — 3,000+ potential buyers.
How Cooperative Purchasing Works
When Riverside County adopts a GovTech solution and places it on a NASPO or OMNIA Partners cooperative contract, the vendor doesn't need to sell to each of the 3,000+ counties in America individually. Any other county can simply reference the Riverside contract and buy directly — same price, same terms, zero new procurement process. This is how a startup tested at WLC can deploy nationally in 12 months.
County Government Procurement
Riverside County fleet management, public works technology, and warehouse operations are direct GovTech challenge customers with real procurement budgets already allocated.
Labor & Workforce Balance
Logistics automation carries real displacement risk. Cohort design includes worker upskilling technology alongside automation — labor partners engaged from day one.
The Teamsters Consideration
The logistics vertical carries genuine labor displacement risk. Warehouse automation and AI routing directly affect Teamsters and SEIU members who make up a significant share of WLC's workforce. RIN proactively engages labor partners before the cohort launches — not after. Cohort challenges are designed to include worker safety, upskilling, and career-pathway technology alongside automation. This makes RIN politically durable in a way a pure automation pitch never could be.
March Air Reserve Base

The Detroit Model
Ford didn't move to every city that needed a car. It built cars in Detroit and shipped them everywhere. The Riverside model is identical — build and prove GovTech solutions here, then deploy them nationally, while keeping the companies, the jobs, and the tax base in Riverside County. The WLC, Cal Fire, and NSWC Corona are the factory floor. The rest of America is the market.
